## What Is Wazohost?

Wazohost is a web hosting and domain registration company based in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania — a product of Wazoefu Technology. They've been in the market since around 2016 and are accredited by TCRA (Tanzania Communications Regulatory Authority) as a domain registrar.

The pitch is simple: local hosting, local payment options, local support. For a small business, school, NGO, or solo entrepreneur in Tanzania who just wants to get a site online without jumping through international hoops, that's a genuinely useful thing.

## What's Actually Good Here

**It's genuinely local.** The company is based in Dar es Salaam, supports Tanzanian payment methods, and registers .TZ domains directly. For local businesses, that matters — you're not emailing a support desk in another timezone when something breaks.

**The pricing is accessible.** Tsh 80,000/year for a full hosting setup with unlimited email and a free domain is competitive by any measure. International hosts might offer cheaper entry-level prices in USD, but once you add currency conversion, international transfer fees, and the absence of local support, the real cost climbs quickly.

**The Silver plan is a very sensible choice for most people.** One website, unlimited email accounts, a free .TZ domain, free SSL, and cPanel. That's 90% of what a small Tanzanian business or personal brand needs.

**99.9% uptime guarantee** is standard these days, but it's worth noting — along with SSD storage across all plans, which keeps sites loading reasonably fast.

## A Few Things to Keep in Mind

The Basic Plan is really quite stripped-down — only 4 email accounts and no free domain. It exists as a budget entry point, but most people will want to step up to Silver.

The storage sizes are modest by global standards (1–20 GB across the range). For most business websites and blogs, that's fine. If you're running a large media site or storing large files, you'd want to think about that.

Third-party review data for Wazohost is limited online, which is typical for smaller regional providers. The company has a social media presence and is listed on directories like WHTop, but extensive independent reviews aren't widely available yet.
